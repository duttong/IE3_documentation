# Power Down and Power Up

Use this procedure when IE3 needs to be fully powered down and brought back online.

## Power Down

1. Close the calibration tank cylinder valves:
   - low calibration tank
   - high calibration tank
   - reference tank
2. Leave the regulators alone. Close only the cylinder valves.
3. Leave the `N2` cylinder on. This keeps the GC clean.
4. Quit the IE3 software by clicking the `X` in either IE3 window:
   - chromatogram window
   - `IE3 Control Panel` window
5. Shut down the Linux computer from the operating system shutdown button in the upper right corner.
6. Turn off the Agilent GC by pressing the green flashing button on the lower left of the instrument.
7. Turn off the UPS on the table behind the computer. This powers down the pumps, computer, and GC valves.

## Power Up

1. Turn on the Agilent GC using the same green button on the lower left of the instrument.
2. Wait a few minutes for the GC to reboot.
3. Open the calibration tank cylinder valves:
   - low calibration tank
   - high calibration tank
   - reference tank
4. Wait about 5 minutes for the GC to stabilize before turning on the other equipment.
5. Turn on the UPS power for the pumps, computer, and valves.
6. The computer should reboot on its own and start the IE3 software.
7. If the software does not start automatically, log in to the IE3 account. For credentials, contact Geoff Dutton at `geoff.dutton@noaa.gov`.
8. Double-click the `IE3 Run` icon on the desktop to start the program.

## Notes

- Do not close the `N2` cylinder during a normal IE3 shutdown.
- Do not change regulator settings as part of the normal power-down procedure.
- Do not put account passwords in this public documentation repository.
- If credentials are needed, contact Geoff Dutton at `geoff.dutton@noaa.gov`.
